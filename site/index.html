First things first

Before anything else: your visa

U.S. passport holders need an Indian e-Visa. It’s a simple online form, but do it early.

  • Apply ONLY at the official government site: indianvisaonline.gov.in. Third-party “visa services” charge triple for the same thing.
  • Choose the 30-day e-Tourist visa (~US$25). Approval typically takes 3–7 days; you’ll print the approval and show it on arrival.
  • Apply by early December. That leaves room for a photo re-upload or a slow week without touching your trip.
  • Your passport must be valid 6 months beyond arrival (through at least July 2027) with 2 blank pages. Check it today; renewals take 6-8 weeks.

Arriving & getting ready

Getting there

One airport: Mangaluru (IXE), where we’ll pick you up

Everyone flies into Mangaluru International (IXE), no matter where you start. Send us your flight in the RSVP and consider ground transport handled. It’s a scenic 3.5–4 hr drive to the resort, and we’ll be there when you land.

Why IXE? It’s a small, easy airport (no mega-terminal chaos for first-time travelers), it has a direct Air India Express flight from Dubai, and it connects by short domestic hop from all three of India’s big international hubs:

  • From Bangalore (BLR): 1-hr hop, ~$30–50, several daily (IndiGo, Air India Express)
  • From Delhi (DEL): ~3 hr nonstop, daily (IndiGo, Air India, Air India Express)
  • From Mumbai (BOM): ~1.5 hr domestic, multiple daily options

Flying in from far away? Our travel guide lays out four ways to break up the trip, but your last flight is always the same: a short hop into IXE, where we’ll be waiting.

Money

  • Currency is the Indian Rupee (₹). Cards work at the resort; carry ~₹5,000 (≈$60) in cash for small stops. Airport ATMs are the easy source.
  • Tell your bank you’re traveling. No need to buy rupees in the U.S.

Phone

  • Easiest: an eSIM (Airalo or similar) bought before you fly. You’ll have working data the minute you land.
  • Physical SIMs at the airport need your passport photo page; fine, just slower.
  • WhatsApp is how India communicates. Install it before you leave; it’s also how you reach us.

Power, weather, packing

  • India uses Type C/D/M plugs, 230V. Pack a universal adapter; your phone charger handles the voltage.
  • January in Coorg: gorgeous. Days 75–80°F, evenings 50–55°F in the hills, so bring a real layer for nights.
  • Comfortable shoes (the resort is 37 acres), sunscreen, insect repellent, any prescription meds in original packaging.

Good to know

  • Tap water: don’t. Bottled/filtered water is everywhere, including the resort.
  • Shoes come off at temples and homes; shoulders/knees covered at religious sites.
  • Head-wobble means yes. Usually.
