Before anything else: your visa

U.S. passport holders need an Indian e-Visa. It’s a simple online form, but do it early.

  • Apply ONLY at the official government site: indianvisaonline.gov.in — third-party “visa services” charge triple for the same thing.
  • Choose the 30-day e-Tourist visa (~US$25). Approval typically takes 3–7 days; you’ll print the approval and show it on arrival.
  • Apply by early December — that leaves room for a photo re-upload or a slow week without touching your trip.
  • Your passport must be valid 6 months beyond arrival (through at least July 2027) with 2 blank pages. Check it today; renewals take 6-8 weeks.

The journey is the adventure

The wedding is a Tuesday–Wednesday in the mountains of India. Here’s how to turn getting there into the trip of a lifetime — four ways to break up the flight, each with 2–3 days somewhere extraordinary. Every route ends the same way: a short hop into Mangaluru (IXE), where we’ll pick you up.

Route 01 — Westbound · Premium

The Dubai Route

US West Coast → Dubai (2–3 days) → nonstop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Fly the westbound way and break the trip in the most theatrical layover city on Earth. Desert safari one day, Burj Khalifa and the souks the next — then a short hop lands you practically at the wedding. Pricier on the ground, but iconic.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Dubai, ~20–24 hr with 1 stop · $800–1,200 round-trip as of July 2026
  • Stopover: 2–3 days — desert safari, Burj Khalifa, beaches, the malls
  • Leg 2: Air India Express nonstop Dubai → IXE, ~4 hr — the only route on this page with a direct international flight to the pickup airport
  • Timezone trick: Dubai is UTC+4, India UTC+5:30 — you do the hard adjustment on the long leg, then barely feel the hop

Book legs 1+2 as one ticket where you can. If they’re separate tickets, leave a full buffer day in Dubai and carry travel insurance. Land in India by Jan 3. And when you land — we’ll pick you up.

Route 02 — Eastbound · Best Value

The Bangkok Route

US West Coast → Bangkok (2–3 days) → Bangalore (BLR) → 1-hr hop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Temples, street food, rooftop nightlife — and your dollar goes further than anywhere else on this page. $30/night hotels, $3 meals that ruin you for home cooking. The best value route by a mile.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Bangkok, ~20–24 hr with 1 stop (via Tokyo/Seoul/Hong Kong) · $600–900 round-trip as of July 2026
  • Stopover: 2–3 days — Grand Palace, Chatuchak market, khao soi you’ll dream about
  • Leg 2: Bangkok → Bangalore (BLR), ~4 hr direct (Thai Airways, IndiGo, Air India)
  • Leg 3: BLR → Mangaluru (IXE), 1 hr, ~$30–50, several daily

Yes, this one has an extra leg — the Bangalore connection. Bangkok is worth it.

Book the long-haul and the India legs as one ticket where you can. Separate tickets? Leave a full buffer day in Bangkok and carry travel insurance. Land in India by Jan 3 — and when you do, we’ll pick you up at IXE.

Route 03 — Eastbound · Whoa

The Tokyo Route

US West Coast → Tokyo (2–3 days) → JAL nonstop to Bangalore (BLR) → 1-hr hop to Mangaluru (IXE)

The shortest first leg on this page and the biggest “whoa” per day. January Tokyo is cool, clear, and calm. Once in a lifetime — and you’re already on this side of the world.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Tokyo, ~14–16 hr total with 1 stop (via SEA/LAX) · $540–900 round-trip as of July 2026 — the shortest first leg of any route
  • Stopover: 2–3 days — food, temples, Shibuya at night, absurdly good convenience stores
  • Leg 2: JAL nonstop Tokyo (NRT) → Bangalore (BLR), ~8 hr, 3× weekly — build your dates around it
  • Leg 3: BLR → Mangaluru (IXE), 1-hr hop

The JAL Bangalore nonstop is only 3× weekly, so lock your dates to it. One ticket beats two; a separate booking wants a buffer day in Tokyo and travel insurance. Land in India by Jan 3 — we’ll be at IXE.

Route 04 — Already in India

The Delhi Route

US West Coast → Delhi (2–3 days) → domestic nonstop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Land in India first, adjust once, and spend your stopover already in the country — with a Taj Mahal day trip (Agra, ~3 hr by train), the Red Fort, and Old Delhi street food.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Delhi, ~20–24 hr with 1 stop · $600–900 round-trip as of July 2026
  • Stopover: 2–3 days, already timezone-adjusted for the wedding
  • Leg 2: Delhi → Mangaluru (IXE), ~2.5–3 hr domestic, multiple daily options — the cheapest final leg of any route

Booking the US–Delhi and Delhi–IXE legs together is simplest. Separate tickets? Leave a buffer day in Delhi. You’re already in India after leg 1, so the last hop is low-stress — and we’ll pick you up at IXE.

Sidebar — for nervous first-timers

The Singapore Option

US West Coast → Singapore (2–3 days) → nonstop to Bangalore (BLR) → 1-hr hop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Never left the country and feeling it? Singapore is training wheels for international travel: everything in English, spotless, hyper-organized, and impossible to get lost in. Singapore Airlines BLR nonstop is ~4.5 hr. Expect $700–1,000 round-trip from the West Coast as of July 2026. Same rules: one ticket where possible, land by Jan 3 — and we’ll pick you up.

Booking windows & the fine print

  • Prices above are round-trip estimates as of July 2026. For early-January travel, fares spike from October — book by Labor Day (Sept 7) and thank yourself later.
  • Not starting from the West Coast? The same stopovers work from anywhere — your final leg is always Mangaluru (IXE), a short domestic hop from Bangalore, Delhi, or Mumbai. Tell us when you land.
  • One ticket beats two. A single itinerary means the airline owns your missed connection. Booking legs separately? Leave a buffer day (not hours) at the transfer city, and buy travel insurance (~$50–80).
  • Aim to be in India by January 3 — two days of slack absorbs any delay and lets you land at the wedding rested.

Getting there & getting ready

One airport: Mangaluru (IXE) — we’ll pick you up

Everyone flies into Mangaluru International (IXE), no matter where you start. Send us your flight in the RSVP and consider ground transport handled — it’s a scenic 3.5–4 hr drive to the resort and we’ll be there when you land.

Why IXE? It’s a small, easy airport (no mega-terminal chaos for first-time travelers), it has a direct Air India Express flight from Dubai, and it connects by short domestic hop from all three of India’s big international hubs:

  • From Bangalore (BLR) — 1-hr hop, ~$30–50, several daily (IndiGo, Air India Express)
  • From Delhi (DEL) — ~2.5 hr domestic, multiple daily options
  • From Mumbai (BOM) — ~1.5 hr domestic, multiple daily options

So whatever your international routing — Dubai, Bangkok, Tokyo, Delhi, Singapore, or anywhere else — your last flight is always the same: a short hop into IXE, where we’ll be waiting.

Money

  • Currency is the Indian Rupee (₹). Cards work at the resort; carry ~₹5,000 (≈$60) in cash for small stops. Airport ATMs are the easy source.
  • Tell your bank you’re traveling. No need to buy rupees in the U.S.

Phone

  • Easiest: an eSIM (Airalo or similar) bought before you fly — working data the minute you land.
  • Physical SIMs at the airport need your passport photo page; fine, just slower.
  • WhatsApp is how India communicates — install it before you leave; it’s also how you reach us.

Power, weather, packing

  • India uses Type C/D/M plugs, 230V — pack a universal adapter (your phone charger handles the voltage).
  • January in Coorg: gorgeous. Days 75–80°F, evenings 50–55°F in the hills — bring a real layer for nights.
  • Comfortable shoes (the resort is 37 acres), sunscreen, insect repellent, any prescription meds in original packaging.
  • Event dress codes are listed with the schedule above.

Good to know

  • Tap water: don’t. Bottled/filtered water is everywhere, including the resort.
  • Shoes come off at temples and homes; shoulders/knees covered at religious sites.
  • Head-wobble means yes. Usually.
