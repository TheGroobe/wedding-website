The journey is the adventure

The wedding is a Tuesday and a Wednesday in the mountains of India. Here’s how to turn getting there into the trip of a lifetime: four ways to break up the flight, each with 2–3 days somewhere extraordinary. Every route ends the same way: a short hop into Mangaluru (IXE).

Route 01 · Westbound · Premium

The Dubai Route

US West Coast → Dubai (2–3 days) → nonstop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Fly the westbound way and break the trip in the most theatrical layover city on Earth. Desert safari one day, Burj Khalifa and the souks the next. Then a short hop lands you practically at the wedding. Pricier on the ground, but iconic.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Dubai, ~$1,050–1,450 round trip with 1 stop for the wedding window (checked July 2026). Emirates’ ~16 hr nonstop runs about $2,100 on these dates
  • Stopover: 2–3 days: desert safari, Burj Khalifa, beaches, the malls
  • Leg 2: Air India Express nonstop Dubai → IXE, ~4 hr, daily. The only route on this page with a direct international flight to IXE
  • Timezone trick: Dubai is UTC+4, India UTC+5:30. You do the hard adjustment on the long leg, then barely feel the hop
  • Check live fares for these dates →

Book legs 1+2 as one ticket where you can. If they’re separate tickets, leave a full buffer day in Dubai and carry travel insurance.

Route 02 · Eastbound · Best Value

The Bangkok Route

US West Coast → Bangkok (2–3 days) → Bangalore (BLR) → 1-hr hop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Temples, street food, rooftop nightlife. Your dollar goes further than anywhere else on this page. $30/night hotels, $3 meals that ruin home cooking for you. The best value route by a mile.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Bangkok, 1 stop (via Seoul/Hong Kong/Taipei), ~20–24 hr · ~$1,100–1,600 round trip for the wedding window (checked July 2026)
  • Stopover: 2–3 days: Grand Palace, Chatuchak market, khao soi you’ll dream about
  • Leg 2: Bangkok → Bangalore (BLR), ~4 hr nonstop (Thai Airways, IndiGo, Air India; ~18 flights a week)
  • Leg 3: BLR → Mangaluru (IXE), 1 hr nonstop, ~$50 even in the wedding week, six-plus daily
  • Check live fares for these dates →

Yes, this one has an extra leg (the Bangalore connection). Bangkok is worth it.

Book the long-haul and the India legs as one ticket where you can. Separate tickets? Leave a full buffer day in Bangkok and carry travel insurance.

Route 03 · Eastbound · Whoa

The Tokyo Route

US West Coast → Tokyo (2–3 days) → JAL nonstop to Bangalore (BLR) → 1-hr hop to Mangaluru (IXE)

The shortest first leg on this page and the biggest “whoa” per day. January Tokyo is cold and clear, with thin crowds. Once in a lifetime, and you’re already on this side of the world.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Tokyo, nonstop from LAX ~12 hr · ~$1,000–1,600 round trip for the wedding window (checked July 2026: ZIPAIR ~$1,010, ANA ~$1,300, JAL ~$1,570). Still the shortest first leg of any route, with the cheapest nonstops
  • Stopover: 2–3 days: food, temples, Shibuya at night, absurdly good convenience stores
  • Leg 2: JAL nonstop Tokyo (NRT) → Bangalore (BLR), ~7.5 hr, daily (goes daily from Aug 2026)
  • Leg 3: BLR → Mangaluru (IXE), 1-hr hop
  • Check live fares for these dates →

The JAL Bangalore nonstop runs daily, so your dates are flexible. One ticket beats two; a separate booking wants a buffer day in Tokyo and travel insurance.

Route 04 · Already in India

The Delhi Route

US West Coast → Delhi (2–3 days) → domestic nonstop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Land in India first, adjust once, and spend your stopover already in the country: a Taj Mahal day trip (Agra, ~3 hr by train), the Red Fort, and Old Delhi street food.

  • Leg 1: West Coast → Delhi, ~20–24 hr with 1 stop · ~$1,350–1,700 round trip for the wedding window (checked July 2026; December and January are peak season on this route)
  • Stopover: 2–3 days, already timezone-adjusted for the wedding
  • Leg 2: Delhi → Mangaluru (IXE), nonstop ~3 hr, daily (IndiGo, Air India, Air India Express)
  • Check live fares for these dates →

Booking both legs on one ticket is simplest. Separate tickets? Leave a buffer day in Delhi. You’re already in India after leg 1, so the last hop is low-stress.

Sidebar · For nervous first-timers

The Singapore Option

US West Coast → Singapore (2–3 days) → nonstop to Bangalore (BLR) → 1-hr hop to Mangaluru (IXE)

Never left the country and feeling it? Singapore is training wheels for international travel: everything in English, spotless, hyper-organized, and impossible to get lost in. Singapore Airlines flies LAX → Singapore nonstop (~17.5 hr) and Singapore → Bangalore nonstop (~4 hr 40 min). Expect ~$1,375–1,900 round trip from the West Coast for the wedding window, or ~$1,950 for the Singapore Airlines nonstop (checked July 2026; check live fares for these dates →). Same rule applies: one ticket where possible.
